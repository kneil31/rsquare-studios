5 Hero Shots The shots that make people stop scrolling

10 Rules

1. YOU GET ONE TAKE. Every camera rolling. Every setting locked. 30 sec before, 2 min after.
2. B-CAM STAYS LOCKED ON FACES. 120p. Tripod. DO NOT TOUCH during reveal.
3. A-CAM = WIDE SAFETY. 15-35mm on gimbal. Even if push-in fails, you have the moment.
4. WIND CHECK 5 MIN BEFORE. Toss grass. Couple INTO wind. Effect TOWARD camera.
5. SUN BEHIND YOUR CAMERA. Never shoot into sun during reveal.
6. NOT EVERYTHING IN SLOW-MO. Keep C-cam at 60p with audio. Reactions without sound = empty.
7. BEAT DROP = EXACT FRAME WHERE COLOR APPEARS. Build entire edit around this sync point.
8. CAPTURE THE SOUNDS. Mic both parents. Plant mic near crowd. Gasps, screams, "IT'S A BOY!" = audio backbone.
9. FILM THE COME-DOWN. Don't pack up after chaos. Belly touch. Quiet smile. Empty prop. = Act 3.
10. IF IT FAILS, KEEP FILMING. Cannon jams. Balloon won't pop. The failure → retry IS a better story.

Interview Techniques (Cheat Sheet)

Silence: After every answer — wait 3-5 sec. Nod warmly. Their second answer is always the real one.
Delayed Cut: After "that's all my questions" — keep rolling 60 sec. "You know what, I didn't say this, but..." = gold.
The Reframe: If rehearsed — "Tell me again, but talk to your baby, not to me." Drops the performance.
Detecting PR: "You feel grateful" (distancing) vs "I feel grateful" (real). Watch for you/I shift.
Hands: B-cam on their hands from the side during interview. Fidgeting, gripping = intercut gold.
Tears: Don't say "I'm sorry." Don't say "we can stop." Nod. Let them feel it. The footage is sacred.
