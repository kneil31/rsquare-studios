Interview — Parents

Quiet corner away from speakers. Lav mic on collar. 7-10 min. Say: "Answer in Telugu, English, or both — whatever feels natural."
Swap DJI TX1 to parent collar (6-8" below chin, windscreen on)
Position B-cam on their hands from the side
ICE: "This was beautiful — how are you feeling right now?"
Don't use in edit. Just let them settle. Nod and smile.
Q1: "Tell me the story of Dhriti's name. How did you land on it — and what does it mean to you?"
INTERCUT: Father whispering name in her ear (120p). Name on rice tray. Akshatalu shower.
Got Q1 answer
Q2: "When the maternal uncle touched the gold ring dipped in milk and honey to her tongue — what was going through your mind in that exact moment?"
INTERCUT: Gold ring moment (120p). Uncle's hands. Dhriti's face tasting. Mom watching from the side.
Got Q2 answer
Q3: "When the women gathered around the cradle and sang the jola pata — was there a moment where it hit you that this is real, she's really here?"
INTERCUT: Women singing around cradle. Cradle swinging (120p). Dhriti being rocked. Hands placing flowers.
Got Q3 answer
Q4: "What's something about Dhriti that nobody else has noticed yet — something only you see because you're with her every day?"
INTERCUT: Close-ups of Dhriti's hands, feet, facial expressions. The micro-details of her body language.
Got Q4 answer
Q5: "There's a moment in the middle of the night — 2 AM, 3 AM — when it's just you and her. No ceremony, no family. What happens in that moment?"
INTERCUT: Dhriti sleeping or being held. Parent's hands cradling her. Quiet, still shots — no ceremony noise.
Got Q5 answer
Q6: "Dhriti is going to watch this film someday. She'll be 18, sitting somewhere you can't imagine yet. Look into this camera and tell her something she needs to hear from the person you are right now — today."
CLOSER. Direct to camera. Locked off. No cutaways. Let the face and words carry it. If they cry — keep rolling. 5 sec silence after.
Got Q6 answer (THE closer)

Interview — Grandparents

Interview FIRST — they tire faster and leave earlier. Seat them comfortably. Have family member sit behind you so they look at a familiar face.
GP1: "Did your parents do this same ceremony for you? What do you remember — or what have they told you about your own naming day?"
INTERCUT: Grandparents during ceremony. Multi-generational gathering wide.
Got GP1 answer
GP2: "When you look at your child holding Dhriti — seeing them become a parent — what does that do to you?"
INTERCUT: Grandparent's hands vs Dhriti's tiny hands. The moment they held her during ceremony.
Got GP2 answer
GP3: "Say something to Dhriti — she'll watch this when she's grown."
Direct to camera. Let them speak in Telugu if that's what comes. Subtitle in post.
Got GP3 answer
